Sam Aaron's Summary

I am passionate about working in teams of smart, motivated people. I treat my work as a craft, and seek opportunities to learn, develop and share skills with others. I believe in the value of open source software, and aim to make significant contributions to such projects.

I see programming primarily as a form of communication and as such am interested in domain specific languages, language oriented programming techniques and the aesthetics of programming languages. I also believe that the pursuit of conceptual efficiency is at least as important as computational efficiency.

I continue to enjoy presenting my thoughts, ideas and work at a number of events and institutions, including The University of Cambridge, QCon London, JAOO, RubyFoo, RubyFools in Copenhagen and Oslo, RailsConf Europe in Berlin and RubyEnRails in Amsterdam.

Specialties

Communicative programming, the aesthetics of programming languages, domain specific languages, interest management, language oriented programming, functional programming, concurrency, Clojure, Ruby, Rails, behaviour driven development, making software readable.
